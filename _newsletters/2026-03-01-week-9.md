      <h2>European Union</h2>

    <div class="subsection">
      <h3>Regulatory Activity</h3>
      
      <div class="article-item">
        <div class="article-title">EU AI Act Annex 3 Schedule Updated with Stricter Compliance Timelines</div>
        <div class="article-summary">
          The European Commission released revised implementation schedules for high-risk AI systems under Annex 3, 
          moving several compliance deadlines forward by 3-6 months. Organizations must submit conformity assessments 
          by April 15, 2026 instead of the previously announced July 1 deadline.
        </div>
        <div class="article-meta">
          <span class="article-source">📰 European Commission</span>
          <span class="article-date">📅 February 28, 2026</span>
        </div>
        <a href="https://ec.europa.eu/ai-act/annex-3-update" class="article-cta">Read more →</a>
      </div>

      <div class="compliance-callout">
        <div class="callout-title">What This Means for You</div>
        <div class="callout-content">
          If your organization develops or deploys high-risk AI systems in the EU, you now have 45 days to submit 
          conformity documentation. Start internal audits immediately and engage with notified bodies for assessment scheduling.
          <br><br>
          <strong>Action items:</strong>
          <ul style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-left: 1.5rem;">
            <li>Review current AI system inventory against Annex 3 classifications</li>
            <li>Schedule conformity assessment with notified body by March 15</li>
            <li>Prepare technical documentation and risk assessments</li>
            <li>Budget for potential external audit costs (€15k-€50k per system)</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="article-item">
        <div class="article-title">EDPB Issues Guidance on AI and GDPR Intersection</div>
        <div class="article-summary">
          The European Data Protection Board published detailed guidance clarifying how existing GDPR provisions 
          apply to AI systems, particularly regarding automated decision-making (Article 22) and data minimization requirements.
          The guidance emphasizes that AI Act compliance does not automatically ensure GDPR compliance.
        </div>
        <div class="article-meta">
          <span class="article-source">📰 EDPB</span>
          <span class="article-date">📅 February 25, 2026</span>

        <a href="https://edpb.europa.eu/ai-gdpr-guidance" class="article-cta">Read more →</a>

      <h3>Federal Developments</h3>
      
      <div class="article-item">
        <div class="article-title">NIST AI Framework v2.0 Comment Period Closing Soon</div>
        <div class="article-summary">
          The National Institute of Standards and Technology is accepting public comments on the proposed AI Risk 
          Management Framework v2.0 through March 15, 2026. The updated framework includes expanded sections on 
          generative AI and foundation model governance, incident reporting requirements, and supply chain risk management.
        </div>
        <div class="article-meta">
          <span class="article-source">📰 NIST</span>
          <span class="article-date">📅 February 27, 2026</span>
        </div>
        <a href="https://nist.gov/ai-framework-v2-comments" class="article-cta">Read more →</a>
      </div>

      <div class="compliance-callout">
        <div class="callout-title">What This Means for You</div>
        <div class="callout-content">
          Review the draft framework and submit comments if your organization uses foundation models or generative AI. 
          The final version will likely shape future federal procurement requirements and industry best practices.
          <br><br>
          Key changes to note:
          <ul style="margin-top: 0.5rem; padding-left: 1.5rem;">
            <li>New mandatory incident reporting for federal contractors</li>
            <li>Foundation model provenance tracking requirements</li>
            <li>Enhanced third-party risk assessment protocols</li>
            <li>Alignment with EU AI Act risk classifications (intentional harmonization)</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="article-item">
        <div class="article-title">Executive Order 14129 Implementation Clarification Released</div>
        <div class="article-summary">
          The Office of Management and Budget (OMB) released detailed implementation guidance for EO 14129 
          on "Safe, Secure, and Trustworthy AI." The memo clarifies federal agency responsibilities for AI inventory 
          management, impact assessments, and public transparency reporting. All agencies must publish AI use registries 
          by June 1, 2026.

        <div class="article-title">California AI Transparency Bill Advances in Committee</div>
        <div class="article-summary">

          including images, video, and audio. Enforcement would begin January 1, 2027 with penalties up to $10,000 per violation.
        </div>

          <span class="article-source">📰 California State Assembly</span>
